# Product Overview

## What DropOS is

DropOS is operational infrastructure for high-demand retail launches. It
sits underneath a retailer's own website and store operations, coordinating
registration, allocation, reservations, QR-based verification, and
launch-day operations from one connected system.

## Who it's for

DropOS is built for organisations that run **limited-access retail
moments**, where demand for a fixed amount of stock or space exceeds
supply on a specific day:

- **Retailers and brands** launching a limited-edition product, collaboration,
  or exclusive release
- **Mall operators** hosting a high-footfall pop-up or brand activation
  across one or more physical locations
- **Activation agencies** running a launch event on behalf of a brand
  client, often across multiple stores or markets
- **Store operations teams** — managers and floor staff — who need
  reliable, mobile-first tools to run the event itself, not just plan it

These are not general e-commerce or generic event-ticketing use cases.
The common thread is a **hard capacity constraint** (a fixed number of
units, slots, or entries) combined with **demand that exceeds it**, on a
specific date, requiring in-person or time-boxed fulfilment.

## The problem, in more depth

A typical high-demand retail launch today is assembled from tools that
were not designed for it:

- A **registration form** (Typeform, Google Forms, or a bespoke landing
  page) captures interest, but has no concept of inventory or eligibility
- A **spreadsheet** becomes the allocation record, maintained manually
  under time pressure
- A **generic ticketing tool**, if used at all, has no concept of a
  "collection window" tied to physical inventory
- **In-store verification** falls back to a printed list or a static QR
  code that can be screenshotted and shared, undermining the entire
  access-control premise

The result is a launch day where staff are reacting rather than operating
from a system of record — and where the retailer has no reliable way to
prove, after the fact, that allocation was fair.

## Current market focus

DropOS is being built and validated against the operational realities of
retail launches in demand-dense markets, with the GCC as an initial focus
area for early pilot conversations.
